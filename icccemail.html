Guild for Blind Client Turns Catholic Charities Superstar

Spotlight on Stephanie Brown


Finding homes for the hardest-to-house homeless—men and women hearing voices, veterans reliving war’s horror, those who spent years on the streets—is Stephanie Brown’s passion and specialty.  Called by Alisa Texeira, her Kennedy Center Preserving Housing Supervisor, “the SEPS guru” after the program aimed at serving this group.

The key to her success?

“There are things I couldn’t see before I lost my vision because I was hearing but not listening,” Stephanie says. “Now I understand people’s struggles and, thanks to Catholic Charities, have the tools to help.”

Stephanie first came to us as a Guild for the Blind client.  She needed help, she said, reviving her once-successful career as an executive assistant before Steven Johnson Syndrome, a rare disorder that blinded her, stole her confidence and career.

Catholic Charities Guild for the Blind Employment Coordinator Tony Severo spotted Stephanie’s skills and wound up marketing her to Catholic Charities.  Lucky for us, she’s been part of our staff since October 2012.

Now a line forms for those eager to tout Stephanie’s skills.

“My wife Karina, our unborn baby boy and I would like to thank you so very much for all the help you offered us,” says Francis P. “You treated us with such attention and love we will never forget that.”
